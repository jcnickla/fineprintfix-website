Employment Agreement High risk

This is an employment contract between you and Acme Technologies Inc. for a Senior Software Engineer role starting January 1, 2025. The agreement contains several restrictive clauses that significantly limit your rights — particularly around non-compete, IP ownership, and dispute resolution.

Aggressive non-compete and IP clauses broader than standard. Mandatory arbitration waives your right to sue in court.
Key facts
PartiesEmployee and Acme Technologies Inc.
Base salary$120,000 per year
Non-compete24 months after employment ends, worldwide
Notice period2 weeks (both parties)
Red flag
Non-compete (§7.1) — 24-month worldwide restriction. You can't work for any competitor anywhere for 2 years after leaving.
Red flag
IP assignment (§6.2) — covers inventions you build on personal time, on weekends, even if unrelated to the company.
Red flag
Mandatory arbitration (§8.1) — waives your right to a jury trial. You also can't join a class action lawsuit.
Ask about
Discretionary bonus (§2.2) — no guaranteed amount, no defined criteria. The company can eliminate it without notice.
Looks fine
Benefits (§3.1) — 15 days PTO, up to 5-day rollover, health insurance from day one. Standard and fair.

$120,000/year base + discretionary bonus

Non-Compete 2 issues
24-month worldwide restriction. Most enforceable non-competes are 6–12 months and limited to a specific region. This is far broader than standard.
Intellectual Property 1 issue
Any project you build on evenings or weekends — even unrelated to the company — belongs to them. Ask for a personal project carve-out.
Benefits & PTO Standard
15 days PTO with standard rollover, health insurance from day one. Nothing to worry about here.

Bring these word-for-word to your negotiation. You don't have to accept everything as written.
