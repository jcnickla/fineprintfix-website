What we flag

NDA red flags that restrict you more than you realize

One-sided vs mutual
Whether both parties are bound by the same confidentiality obligations — or just you.
No expiration date
NDAs with no end date that could restrict you indefinitely — even if the relationship ends quickly.
Overly broad definition
Confidentiality clauses so broad they cover information you'd have no way of knowing was considered secret.
Injunctive relief clauses
Language allowing the other party to seek a court order against you without proving actual financial damages.
Geographic scope
Whether restrictions apply in your jurisdiction and whether they overlap with any non-compete you already have.
Remedies and penalties
What happens if you accidentally breach — and whether liquidated damages are specified or open-ended.
Return of information
Whether you're required to destroy all confidential information and certify deletion — even from memory.
Standard exclusions
Carve-outs for publicly available information and information you independently developed — these should always be present.
Negotiation scripts

Questions to ask before you sign

Even pre-interview NDAs are often negotiable. Here's what to ask:

1
"Can this be made mutual — so both parties are bound by the same confidentiality obligations?"
2
"Can we add an expiration date — for example, 3 years from the date of signing?"
3
"Can we narrow the definition of confidential information to material that's explicitly marked as confidential?"
4
"Can we remove the injunctive relief clause or require proof of irreparable harm before seeking a court order?"
5
"Can we confirm this NDA doesn't restrict me from using general skills or knowledge I developed independently?"
